# Style

Modern LinkedIn writing should feel professionally sharp, useful, and human.

## Desired Tone

- Clear
- Specific
- Observant
- Generous
- Calmly opinionated

## Tone To Avoid

- Inflated
- Sermonic
- Hyper-inspirational
- Self-congratulatory
- Over-optimized for engagement

## Style Principles

## 1. Prefer Specifics Over Status

Weak:

- We are redefining the future of event experiences.

Stronger:

- We redesigned the attendee flow because too many guests were waiting five minutes just to reach registration.

## 2. Prefer Insight Over Hype

Weak:

- Huge things are coming.

Stronger:

- We learned that attendees engaged more when session descriptions explained the concrete outcome, not just the topic.

## 3. Prefer Reader Relevance Over Personal Performance

Weak:

- I am proud to announce...

Stronger:

- If you run events, this change may save your team hours during launch week.

## 4. Prefer Compression Over Drama

- Remove filler adjectives.
- Cut duplicate emphasis.
- Let the example do the persuasive work.

## 5. Sound Human, Not Casual By Default

- Contractions are fine.
- Plain language is better than jargon.
- Slang should be rare unless the audience clearly expects it.

## Trust Builders

- Numbers with context
- Real examples
- Acknowledged trade-offs
- Mechanisms that explain why something worked
- Concrete audience definition

## Trust Killers

- Fake vulnerability
- Unverifiable superlatives
- Generic “game changer” language
- Thought-leadership clichés
- Advice with no proof, pattern, or example
