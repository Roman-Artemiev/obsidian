# Structure

The best LinkedIn structure is readable at a glance and still coherent when read closely.

## Default Structure

`hook -> context -> insight -> implication -> CTA`

This is the safest default for most professional posts.

## Paragraph Rhythm

- Keep paragraphs short enough for mobile reading.
- Use 1-3 sentences per paragraph in most cases.
- Do not break every sentence into its own paragraph unless emphasis is truly needed.
- If the post feels choppy, combine adjacent lines into fuller paragraphs.

## Recommended Body Patterns

### 1. Insight Post

- Hook
- Brief setup
- Main insight
- Why it matters
- Reader takeaway or question

### 2. Story Post

- Hook with tension
- Situation
- Decision or shift
- Lesson
- Reader implication

### 3. Framework Post

- Hook
- Problem statement
- 3-5 steps or principles
- Closing synthesis
- CTA

### 4. Promotion Post

- Hook around the problem or opportunity
- Why this matters now
- What is happening
- Who it is for
- Clear next step

## What Usually Needs Cutting

- Warm-up lines
- Repetition of the same insight in different words
- Long autobiographical setup
- Broad industry clichés
- A second conclusion after the main conclusion

## CTA Rules

- Use one primary CTA.
- Make the CTA match the goal.
- Prefer concrete asks like `Comment "guide" and I'll send it`, `Register here`, or `What would you add?`
- Avoid empty asks like “Thoughts?”

## Readability Test

Before finalizing, check:

- Can someone understand the topic in the first two lines?
- Can they extract the takeaway without reading every sentence?
- Does each paragraph earn its place?
- Does the post still read smoothly if line breaks are reduced?
