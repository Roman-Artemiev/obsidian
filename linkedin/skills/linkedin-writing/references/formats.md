# Formats

Choose the format before drafting. Do not force every idea into a standard post.

## Standard Post

Use when:

- One core idea can be delivered quickly
- The goal is conversation, visibility, or a compact lesson
- The draft fits comfortably under `3,000` characters

Best for:

- Opinions
- Short lessons
- Founder/operator observations
- Event promotion
- Hiring updates

## Article

Use when:

- The topic needs more development than a post allows
- The reader needs depth, examples, or argumentation
- Search value matters more than instant feed performance

Best for:

- Deep explainers
- Detailed breakdowns
- Essays with multiple sections

## Newsletter

Use when:

- The topic can sustain repeated editions
- The audience benefits from subscribing
- You are building a recurring editorial promise

Best for:

- Ongoing industry analysis
- Weekly lessons
- Curated commentary

## Document Post

Use when:

- The content is easier to consume as slides or pages
- The value comes from frameworks, checklists, diagrams, templates, or event information
- You want users to stay in a native multi-page asset

Best for:

- Frameworks
- Playbooks
- Event agendas
- Checklists
- Carousels

## Event Promotion Heuristic

Use a standard post when the event pitch is simple and the goal is quick awareness or registration.

Use a document post when the audience needs agenda detail, speaker context, logistics, or a multi-step takeaway that earns multiple frames.

## Document Post / Carousel Heuristic

Use a document post when the idea gets stronger through sequencing or faster visual scanning.

If you call it a carousel, treat that as the slide-based version of a document post, with one idea per frame.

Do not turn plain text into a document post unless each frame adds distinct value.

## Poll

Use when:

- The goal is lightweight interaction or audience input
- The choices themselves reveal something useful

Do not use when:

- You already know the answer
- The poll is only bait for reach

## Format Selection Heuristic

Ask:

1. Is the value best delivered as text, slides, or recurring editions?
2. Does the idea need depth or speed?
3. Is the goal conversation, education, subscription, or conversion?
4. Will the audience get the value without leaving LinkedIn?
