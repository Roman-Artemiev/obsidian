# LinkedIn Hooks

## Overview

Use this skill when the opening is the problem.

The goal is not to sound dramatic. The goal is to create immediate relevance and enough tension to earn the next lines.

## Workflow

1. Identify the audience and the exact professional problem.
2. Identify the payoff:
   what the reader will learn, avoid, understand, or do.
3. Choose the right hook shape:
   contrarian truth, specific observation, outcome with tension, mistake/friction, or sharp lesson.
4. Draft `5-10` hooks before picking one.
5. Reject any hook that overpromises or could belong on any platform.
6. Ensure the body pays off the opening quickly.

## Rules

- Make the topic legible fast.
- Prefer specificity over hype.
- Prefer credibility over shock value.
- Avoid biography-first openings unless the story itself is the point.
- If the hook takes too long to understand, compress it.

## Output Pattern

- Provide `3-5` hook options.
- Label each by type.
- Mark the recommended hook.
- State in one short line why it is strongest.

## Common Mistakes

- “I’m excited to share...”
- “Here’s what happened...”
- “Content is changing...”
- curiosity with no concrete payoff
- tension that the body cannot support
